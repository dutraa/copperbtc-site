Copper-Backed BTC (cuBTC) is a proof-of-metal experiment on Base that links verifiable copper lots to a satoshi-denominated ERC-20 token.

Ticker: cuBTC Network: Base mainnet Unit: 0-decimal, 1 token = 1 satoshi

What is cuBTC?

Each cuBTC is denominated in satoshis, but backed by attested copper lots proven via:

  • IPFS-hosted receipt folders (photos, invoices, storage info, receipt.json)
  • An on-chain Receipt NFT (ERC-721) that encodes weight, purity, and IPFS URI
  • An on-chain Attestation Registry that stores keccak256(CID), attestor, and timestamp

Experimental RWA project. Not an investment product, not Bitcoin, and not a claim on BTC. cuBTC is denominated in satoshis but backed by real-world copper exposure.

Mission

Copper is the quiet backbone of electrification — power grids, EVs, data centers, renewable energy infrastructure, and everything that moves electrons across the physical world.

Bitcoin, on the other hand, is the native money of the internet — programmable, permissionless, and settled in units of satoshis.

Why copper?

  • Industrial metal with deep real-world demand
  • Critical to grid build-out, EVs, and renewables
  • Long-term macro story tied to electrification

Why Bitcoin-denominated?

  • Each cuBTC maps to 1 satoshi worth of copper-backed exposure
  • Bitcoin-native mental model: stack satoshis, but with industrial metal backing
  • Transparent, programmable bridge between physical metals and digital value

Goal: Build a transparent, programmable bridge between industrial metals and digital value — starting with copper, denominated in satoshis.

How it works (Phase-0)

Phase-0 is intentionally simple: one self-custodied copper lot, fully documented and proven on-chain. Here’s the lifecycle:

  1. 1. Buy & document real copper

    Acquire physical copper (e.g., bars, rounds, industrial lots). Capture invoices, serial numbers, photos, storage details, and any relevant certificates.

  2. 2. Upload to IPFS

    Bundle the evidence into a folder with a canonical receipt.json file and pin it to IPFS (e.g., via Pinata). This folder becomes the source of truth for that copper lot.

  3. 3. Mint a Receipt NFT

    Mint an ERC-721 Receipt NFT on Base pointing to the IPFS URI. Metadata encodes weight, purity, serials, and other key attributes of the lot.

  4. 4. Attest in the Registry

    An on-chain Attestation Registry stores keccak256(CID), attestor address, and timestamp. This creates a cryptographic binding between the NFT and the IPFS folder.

  5. 5. Mint cuBTC (0-decimal ERC-20)

    cuBTC can only be minted for lots that have a valid on-chain attestation. Each minted cuBTC is a 0-decimal token: 1 cuBTC = 1 satoshi-denominated unit.

  6. 6. Verify through the CopperBTC dapp

    The dapp (and open-source scripts) let anyone verify the Receipt NFT, attestation, and CID hash match, and follow links to Basescan, IPFS gateways, and other explorers.

Self-Custodied Ecosystem

Phase-0 is explicitly self-custodied: the copper lot is owned, stored, and insured by the individual, who then proves it cryptographically on-chain.

The idea is simple: Own the metal. Prove it on-chain.

Today: Self-custodied lots

  • You acquire and store copper yourself (home, vault, safe, etc.).
  • You generate receipts, photos, and documentation and upload them to IPFS.
  • You mint a Receipt NFT and register an attestation that binds the CID to your lot.

Tomorrow: Vault programs

  • Integrate with professional vaulting providers (e.g., Money Metals or others) so users can opt into fully managed storage.
  • Vaults issue cryptographic receipts and standardized receipt.json files for each lot.
  • Users can choose between home storage and professional vaults, while still getting the same on-chain proofs.

Roadmap

cuBTC is intentionally shipped as a small, auditable prototype — with a clear path toward stronger attestations, vault integrations, and cross-chain usage.

Phase-0 – Prototype (Today)

  • Single self-custodied copper lot
  • Receipt NFT + Attestation Registry on Base
  • Owner-gated cuBTC minting
  • Explorer / dapp with CID hash verification

Phase-1 – Decentralized Attestation

  • EIP-712 off-chain signatures from recognized attestors
  • Anyone can submit signed attestations on-chain
  • Standardized receipt.json schema for lots

Phase-2 – Chainlink PoR & Vault Integrations

  • Chainlink Proof of Reserve feeds for copper lots
  • Multisig attestor committees with rotation rules
  • Integration with vault programs (e.g., Money Metals, others)
  • Automated freshness, storage, and audit checks

Phase-3 – Redemption & Cross-Chain cuBTC

  • Clear redemption rules for eligible, redeemable lots
  • BTC-denominated reward / accounting logic
  • Cross-chain cuBTC via Chainlink CCIP
  • Institutional-grade reporting, proofs, and audits
